Superconductors

Superconductors sound like they are straight out of science fiction. They are materials that conduct electricity with 0 resistance. If that doesn't blow your mind, I don't know what will. Back in 1911 when they were discovered they shook the scientific community cus everyone realized how powerful they were. Superconductors can be used in levitation, flying cars, and even quantum computers.

So if they are so cool, why don't we have them everywhere? The issue is superconducting will only occur when the material is cooled to a specific temperature called the critical temperature. Usually, these temperatures are very low, like near absolute 0 low. They essentially are impractical to use outside of a lab. However, the hunt is on for a superconductor that exists at a higher temperature. We've discovered thousands of superconductors, but are still yet to discover one at room temperature.

Assuming a room-temperature superconductor even exists, it's likely we will never discover it. The discovery process for them is guessing and checking different materials. Even worse, you need to not only guess what materials work but the temperature and pressure they conduct at. This task is very slow. Luckily, AI is here for the rescue.

I got a dataset of 16,000 superconductors and their critical temperature. Using a generative model called a Variational Auto Encoder we can generate thousands of possible candidate molecules. My program then cross-references the candidate molecules with a data set of real molecules to make sure they exist in the real world. A second model then takes the ones that do exist and evaluates their critical temperature. The only thing left to do is cross our fingers and hope we find some new superconductors!
